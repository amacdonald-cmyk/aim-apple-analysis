Digital Distribution:
Current State

An operational review of AIM's digital subscription distribution — covering cost structure, platform economics, and the fragmentation problems that are making this business hard to manage and harder to optimize.

Issue 01 · Costs
Costs aren't mapped to revenue
Distribution platform fees (Textalk CDP, Zinio) are paid at the title and channel level, but revenue attribution is inconsistent. Some titles are net-negative and it's not visible in any single view.
Issue 02 · Revenue
Revenue is fragmented across 11+ partners
Consumer, institutional, pool-based, and transactional models all report differently, pay on different schedules, and have different economics. No unified P&L view exists.
Issue 03 · Structure
Distribution responsibilities aren't owned
Contracts, platform access, content delivery, and financial reporting span multiple teams without clear ownership. Reporting is manual, inconsistent, and prone to gaps.

Key finding: Zinio Pro fees are the primary drag
15 of 22 Pro titles don't cover their monthly license fee. The platform nets +$5,541 only because four Taunton titles (Fine Woodworking, Fine Homebuilding, Fine Gardening, Threads) generate 87% of all Apple revenue. Without them, the original 18-title contract is –$21,065 over the period.
!
Key finding: EBSCO cost exceeds EBSCO revenue for 10 titles
Upload costs to EBSCO run $100/title/month. 10 titles return less than that in EBSCO revenue. Cutting those titles from EBSCO saves ~$1,000/month and gives up $68 in revenue. Net positive, but requires coordination with Textalk.
