02 · Key findings
Concentration risk

Apple (iTunes) represents 46% of total digital revenue Jan–Apr 2026. Two channels — Apple + Zinio — account for 68% of total. The eMags transition directly touches both.

EBSCO is the structural gap

EBSCO/Flipster generates ~$20k/yr and is not listed in eMags' distribution partner network. This is the one channel that could create a coverage gap in the transition. Needs a direct answer from eMags before any go/no-go decision.

Three channels aren't worth active migration work

PressReader (~$5k), Google Play (~$5k bundled with app), and Magzter (~$1.6k) collectively represent about 7% of annualized revenue. PressReader and Magzter can be evaluated at contract renewal; Google Play comes along with the eMags app solution.

Cengage is independent — not part of this evaluation

Fixed-fee academic licensing deal. Unaffected by the eMags transition. Revenue cadence unclear (one payment Jan–Apr; may be annual or semi-annual). Worth clarifying internally.
