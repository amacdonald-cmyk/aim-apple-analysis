CDP cost exceeds CDP revenue by 6:1
$1,790 in platform fees returned $261 in revenue from these channels Jan–Apr 2026. A net of –$1,529 over four months. The platform's value isn't captured here — Apple and Cengage revenue flowing through titles on the CDP aren't included — but the channel-specific economics are poor.
!
EBSCO upload cost exceeds EBSCO revenue for most titles
Upload cost runs ~$100/title/month. Only 2 titles returned any EBSCO revenue ($100 and $12). 10+ titles likely incur EBSCO upload cost without returning EBSCO revenue. Cutting those saves ~$1,000/month and gives up ~$68.
→
PressReader is the only channel earning at a meaningful rate
$137 over 4 months — spotty reporting (Jan and Apr only) but the highest-yielding CDP channel. Military Trader and World Coin News are the top earners. Audience skews toward collector/enthusiast verticals.
!
Readly and Magzter Gold are effectively zero
Combined $9.28 Jan–Apr across 5 titles. These are pool-based models — revenue depends on AIM's relative read share vs. the full platform catalog. At current volume, these channels don't justify their portion of CDP costs. Consider whether titles should remain active on these platforms.
Methodology Notes
1.Cost = Textalk Media invoices 7564 / 7633 / 7786 / 7896, total $1,790 Jan–Apr 2026. Prorated by channel per issue at $10/channel in prior model.
2.Revenue = AIM net receipts from CDP-distributed channels only (Readly, PressReader, Magzter, Magzter Gold, EBSCO). Excludes Apple, Cengage, Zinio, Google, Barnes & Noble — those channels are not distributed via Textalk CDP.
3.Magzter revenue = AIM's 50% net share per contract. Magzter direct + Magzter Gold combined.
4.The CDP platform also handles content conversion and delivery for Apple and other channels — that value is not captured in this analysis. This view isolates the marginal revenue from the CDP-specific distribution channels only.
5.Period: January–April 2026 only. Not annualized.
