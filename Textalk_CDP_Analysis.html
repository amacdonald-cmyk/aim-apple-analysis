04 Key Findings
↑
Platform generates strong return over 12 months
5.8× return on $5,400 in distribution cost. $25,753 net across Jun 2025–May 2026. Platform economics are sound — optimization focus is on tail titles and EBSCO allocation.
★
Readly remains the dominant channel
$13,624 over 12 months — 44% of all Textalk-channel revenue. Broad title coverage. Woodsmith is the top earner. Consistent month-to-month with no significant drop-offs.
!
EBSCO cost concentration remains the key issue
EBSCO carries the majority of per-issue upload cost but $4,892 in 12-month revenue. The EBSCO-only collector/hobby titles (Numismatic News, Old Cars Weekly, Sports Collectors Digest, etc.) generate minimal EBSCO returns but incur full upload cost per issue.
→
Barnes & Noble and PressReader are consistent mid-tier channels
$8,033 and $3,108 respectively over 12 months. Garden Gate and Cuisine at Home drive B&N. PressReader is spread across more titles.
→
Magzter contribution is marginal
$1,497 combined (direct + Gold) over 12 months — 4.8% of total. Positive but well below every other channel. Worth monitoring whether continued inclusion in issue uploads is justified.
!
Dec 2025 and Apr 2026 are revenue low points
December at $1,184 and April at $1,876 — both well below the 12-month average of $2,596/mo. Seasonality or issue cadence worth monitoring, especially if Dec cost ($250) is the annual low while April cost ($360) is not.
Methodology Notes
  1. Period: June 2025 – May 2026 (12 months). Cost sourced from Textalk invoice history. Revenue sourced from AIM digital subscriptions revenue report.
  2. Revenue excludes Apple, Apple News, Zinio, Cengage, and Google — channels not distributed through the Textalk platform. Scoped to 17 Textalk-distributed titles only.
  3. 2025 revenue pulled from the December 2025 YTD cumulative report. 2026 revenue from the May 2026 YTD cumulative report.
  4. July 2025 cost ($440, invoice 6873) included. July 2025 revenue sourced from the December 2025 file (Jan–Dec YTD).
  5. ROI by channel uses revenue-proportional cost allocation, not direct channel cost assignment (Textalk invoices are per-issue, not per-channel).
  6. Magzter revenue = AIM's 50% net share per contract (direct + Gold combined).
