02 Cost Structure — Two Billing Layers

Zinio Pro invoices cover two distinct cost components. The Pro Services license fee is fixed monthly per title regardless of usage. Content conversion is charged per issue on implementation. Combined they represent the full invoice amount.

Apple Pro app revenue flows to AIM directly — Apple's ~30% platform fee is deducted before remittance. This revenue does not appear on the Zinio invoice. AIM retains all app revenue and manages the Apple relationship independently.

05 Key Findings
!
Zinio net never covers its own invoice
Across all 12 months, Zinio publisher net remittance averaged $4,163/month against an average invoice of $7,534/month. That's 55¢ recovered per dollar spent — a structural shortfall, not a cyclical one. July 2025 was the worst: $1,575 net against a $7,246 invoice.
★
Four titles generate 86% of Apple revenue
Fine Woodworking, Fine Homebuilding, Fine Gardening, and Threads combined generated $71,488 in Apple app revenue over 12 months — 86% of the total $83,488 from the top 8 titles. These four are why the platform is net-positive. Their Pro fees total $45,600/yr against $71,488 in Apple revenue — a 1.6× return.
!
Remove Apple and Zinio is –$40,450 over 12 months
The platform is entirely dependent on Apple Pro app subscriptions to make the economics work. The Zinio channel itself — Store, Library, and distribution — generates $49,953 against $90,403 in invoiced costs. That's a –$40,450 loss before Apple is factored in.
→
Fixed fee structure disconnected from performance
Pro license rates are fixed at $200–$400/title/month regardless of subscriber count, revenue, or usage. Kovels Antique Trader paid $2,400 in Pro fees over 12 months and generated zero Apple revenue. There is no contractual mechanism to reduce fees when titles underperform.
Methodology Notes
  1. Invoice data: actual Zinio billing records (SI-46245 through SI-58577), May 2025–Apr 2026. April 2026 invoice ($7,780.84) is unpaid as of reporting date.
  2. Zinio net revenue: monthly publisher remittance from the Dig Subs Rev by Partner sheet across all uploaded revenue files. 2025 months sourced from the December 2025 cumulative file; 2026 months from the May 2026 cumulative file.
  3. Apple revenue: net receipts after Apple's ~30% platform fee, from the same revenue files. Includes all AIM Pro app titles. Marine titles (Sail, PassageMaker, Soundings, Anglers Journal, Power & Motoryacht, Yachts International) excluded — divested.
  4. Pro Services Fee: confirmed billing schedule — 16 titles at $200–$400/month = $3,800/month total. Title-level Apple revenue in Section 03 is estimated from Jan–Apr 2026 actuals annualized; actual 12-month by-title breakdown not available.
  5. Content conversion cost (Section 02): derived as invoice total minus Pro fee total ($90,403 – $45,600 = $44,803). Actual per-invoice breakdown not available.
  6. Zinio Store channel revenue ($32.48 for Jan–Apr 2026) is de minimis and not modeled separately in the 12-month view.
