01 Cost Structure — Two Billing Layers

Zinio Pro carries two distinct cost components billed separately. The Pro Services fee is a fixed monthly charge per title regardless of usage or revenue. Content conversion (Schedule A, Appendix A) is a per-issue fee charged on implementation.

04 Key Findings
★
Four titles carry the platform
Fine Woodworking, Fine Homebuilding, Fine Gardening, and Threads generate $27,162 in Apple revenue over 4 months against $5,900 in combined Pro fees — a 4.6× return, accounting for 87% of all Pro platform Apple revenue. Without these four titles the platform nets –$21,065 over the same period.
!
15 of 22 titles don't cover their Pro fee
The original 18-title contract was built around titles that, with limited exceptions, never generated enough app revenue to justify the fixed monthly license. Of the original 18, only Popular Woodworking, Writer's Digest, and Woodsmith are net positive. The remaining 15 titles collectively spend $16,000 in Pro fees over 4 months to generate $4,544 in Apple revenue.
!
Marine titles are the worst performers
Soundings ($400/mo fee, $122 rev), PassageMaker ($400/mo, $93 rev), Anglers Journal ($300/mo, $0 rev), Power & Motoryacht ($300/mo, $0 rev), and Yachts International ($250/mo, $27 rev) collectively cost $8,200 in Pro fees over 4 months and return $242. That's a 97% loss rate. All five are candidates for immediate Pro exit.
→
Conversion costs compound losses for high-frequency titles
Numismatic News (32 issues/year at $80/issue) averages $213/month in conversion fees on top of a $200 Pro fee — against $24/month in Apple revenue. Old Cars Weekly (24 issues/year at $60/issue) and Antique Trader (18 issues/year at $80/issue) face the same math. For these titles, even eliminating the Pro fee doesn't get to breakeven — conversion costs alone exceed revenue.
→
Zinio Store revenue is effectively zero
The revenue report shows $32.48 in Zinio Store channel revenue for all 22 titles over 4 months — two SIP titles only. The platform generates no meaningful Zinio channel revenue beyond Apple. This means cost reduction through Pro exits doesn't sacrifice any material Zinio distribution revenue, only the Apple app subscription income from those specific titles.
!
The Pro fee structure is fixed and unrelated to performance
Monthly rates range from $200 to $400 per title regardless of subscriber count, revenue, or MAU. The MAU cap (50,000/month) is almost certainly never approached for most titles. There is no contractual mechanism that reduces the fee when a title underperforms. Every month a loss-making title stays on Pro is a sunk cost with no recovery path.
Methodology Notes
  1. Pro Services Fee = contracted monthly rate per title, fixed regardless of MAU or revenue. 22 titles total. Total monthly Pro fee: $5,900.
  2. Content Conversion Fee = per-issue rate from Appendix A × contracted issues/year, annualized and divided by 12, then ×4. Fine Woodworking, Fine Homebuilding, Fine Gardening use 7 issues/yr; Threads uses 5 issues/yr. Actual monthly amounts vary by publication calendar. Numismatic News conversion cost is high due to 32 issues/year at $80/issue.
  3. Apple revenue = net receipts from the Jan–Apr 2026 digital subscriptions revenue report. Apple's ~30% platform fee has already been deducted — figures are AIM's net.
  4. Zinio Store channel revenue ($32.48 total for the period) is excluded from the title P&L as it is governed by the base Distribution Channels agreement (Schedule B), not the Pro Services schedule (Schedule C). It is not affected by Pro exits.
  5. Channel delivery fees ($0.10/general delivery, $0.05/library delivery per the base contract) are not modeled — actual delivery volume data is unavailable. These would add incremental cost.
  6. Period: January–April 2026 only. Not annualized. Scenario savings of +$4,000/mo represent the ongoing run-rate benefit from exiting Pro on 15 titles.
